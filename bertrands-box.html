How does this work?

There are three boxes: one has two gold coins, one has two silver coins, and one has one gold and one silver.

You draw a coin at random. If it's gold, what's the probability the other coin in the same box is also gold?

Most people say 1/2 -- reasoning there are two boxes with gold (the GG box and the GS box), so it's 50-50. But the answer is actually 2/3.

The key insight: there are 3 gold coins total. Two of them are in the Gold/Gold box, and only one is in the Gold/Silver box. So given you drew a gold coin, 2 out of 3 times you drew from the Gold/Gold box -- meaning the other coin is gold.
