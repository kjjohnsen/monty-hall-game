The Birthday Paradox

Why does this happen?

The Birthday Paradox shows that in a group of just 23 people, there's a ~50.7% chance two share the same birthday. With 50 people, it jumps to 97%!

The surprise comes from counting pairs, not people. We often think of comparing our own birthday to everyone else's (which requires 183 people to cross 50%). But here, we are comparing everyone to everyone else.

With k people there are k(k-1)/2 pairs to compare. At 23 people that's 253 pairs -- plenty of chances for a match.

The exact probability of no match with k people is:

(365/365) × (364/365) × (363/365) × ... × ((365-k+1)/365)

This product shrinks faster than intuition suggests, crossing 50% right around k = 23.
