How does this work?

Behind one door is a car; the rest have goats. You pick a door, then the host (who knows what's behind each door) opens N−2 other doors that all have goats. You then choose: stay with your original pick or switch to the one remaining unopened door.

With N doors, staying wins with probability 1/N, while switching wins with probability (N−1)/N. The more doors, the bigger the advantage of switching!
