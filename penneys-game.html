Penney's Game

Both players pick a 3-flip coin sequence. Flip until one appears. Since coins are fair, it should be 50/50. Pick first and see what happens.

Before you play — is this game fair?

Player 1 picks any 3-flip sequence (e.g., H-H-H). Player 2 then picks a different sequence. A coin is flipped repeatedly until one player's sequence appears in the stream — that player wins. Both players use the same fair coin. Player 2 gets to see Player 1's choice before picking their own.

How does this work?

In Penney's Game, two players each choose a sequence of three coin flips (e.g., HTH). A coin is flipped repeatedly until one of the sequences appears.

Paradoxically, no matter what sequence Player 1 picks, Player 2 can always pick a different sequence that has a higher probability of appearing first. This makes the game non-transitive (like Rock-Paper-Scissors).

The optimal strategy for Player 2 against Player 1's sequence (1st, 2nd, 3rd) is: (Opposite of 2nd), (1st), (2nd). This guarantees Player 2 wins between 66% and 87% of the time, depending on Player 1's choice!
