How does this work?

The Secretary Problem (also called the Optimal Stopping Problem) asks: how do you maximize your chance of picking the single best candidate when you must evaluate them one at a time and make an irrevocable accept/reject decision on each?

The optimal strategy is to reject the first K candidates (the "exploration phase"), then hire the next person who is better than everyone you've seen so far. The magic number is K = floor(N/e), where e is Euler's number (~2.718).

With this strategy, your probability of hiring the absolute best candidate converges to 1/e ~ 36.8% regardless of N. This is remarkably good given that random guessing yields only 1/N.

The "Sweep K" chart shows that success rate peaks near K = floor(N/e) and falls off on both sides -- too little exploration and you don't know what's good; too much and you may miss the best candidate.
