The Sleeping Beauty Problem

Sleeping Beauty is woken up once if a coin is heads, and twice if it's tails, with her memory erased in between. When she wakes, what is her subjective probability that the coin landed heads? 1/2 or 1/3?

How does this work?

On Sunday, a fair coin is flipped. Sleeping Beauty is put to sleep.

If Heads: she is woken on Monday, interviewed, then the experiment ends.

If Tails: she is woken on Monday, interviewed, her memory is erased, then she is woken again on Tuesday and interviewed again. She cannot tell which day it is.

Each time she wakes, she is asked: "What is your credence that the coin landed Heads?"

The Halfer says 1/2: the coin is fair, and waking provides no new evidence. Measured as: heads experiments / total experiments.

The Thirder says 1/3: there are three equally likely waking-states (Heads-Monday, Tails-Monday, Tails-Tuesday), and only one is heads. Measured as: heads wakings / total wakings.

Both positions are defensible. The disagreement is about what "credence" means -- leading to one of the most debated problems in philosophy of probability.
